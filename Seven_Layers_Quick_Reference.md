## The 7 Layers

| # | Layer | What it covers | Where programmes get stuck |
|---|-------|---------------|---------------------------|
| 0 | **Economic Viability** | Does this asset need to be tokenised? Operating model, team, budget confirmed. | Teams skip Layer 0 and discover no investor demand — or the wrong operating model — post-launch |
| 1 | **Asset** | Due diligence, legal ownership, SPV formation, valuation, banking screening starts | Hidden encumbrances at notarial stage — after technical work has started |
| 2 | **Legal & Regulatory** | Token classification, CASP assessment, jurisdictional analysis, wallet policy | Wrong token class = full architectural redesign. CASP: 3–6 months, never on the plan |
| 3 | **Tokenisation** | Smart contract design and audit, token standard, whitelist, oracle, Proof of Reserves | Audit finding post-code-freeze = new audit cycle. PoR not in original architecture = migration |
| 4 | **Financial Infrastructure** | SPV banking, custody model, settlement, ISO 20022, KYC/AML operational | Bank refuses SPV — programme stops. Custody decided too late to change |
| 5 | **Identity & Compliance** | Investor onboarding live, CASP confirmed, issuance gate (4 sign-offs) | Onboarding bottleneck = go-live blocker. Revenue delayed 3–6 months |
| 6 | **Market & Liquidity** | Secondary market live, market maker active, banking operational, PoR confirmed | Platform launches without buyers. Investors cannot exit. Trust collapses |
| 7 | **Governance & Reporting** | Decision rights, governance charter, audit trail, hypercare — **runs across ALL layers from day one** | Governance vacuum post-launch. No clear owner when things go wrong |

> *"The most dangerous assumption in RWA delivery is: 'We will address that later.' In a seven-layer programme, 'later' always means 'after a dependency we didn't see has already created a delay.'"*

## Layer 0 — Gate Detail

**Entry question:** Is there a documented economic case with sustainable investor demand, viable revenue model, and operating model confirmed?

**Where it gets stuck:** Teams build technically perfect platforms with no investor demand. Second most common failure: wrong operating model discovered in Layer 3 — full programme restart required.

**Gate — cannot proceed to Layer 1 without:**
1. Business case approved — CEO, CFO, Board
2. Operating model confirmed with rationale
3. Token type decision initiated — legal opinion commissioned
4. CASP/VASP strategy confirmed — who holds licence, which NCA, what timeline
5. Team structure confirmed — in-house vs external, TVL scaling threshold
6. Budget approved — initial and Year 1 operating costs

## Three Risks That Decide Program Success

**Banking rejection.** Some banks refuse SPVs connected to blockchain activities. Screen minimum 3 banks simultaneously from Week 1 — never a single banking relationship. Banking screening takes 8–20 weeks. The clock starts from first contact, not from complete application.

**Smart contract exploit.** Pause via multi-sig immediately — no committee approval needed. NCA notification within 4 hours (DORA). Two independent audits before go-live are not optional. One audit is not enough.

**KYC/AML bottleneck.** Onboarding is not a feature — it is the critical path. Teams that treat KYC/AML as a technical item consistently underestimate time and cost. Define investor tier SLAs before the first API call is written.
